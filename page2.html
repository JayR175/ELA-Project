What is Social Media?

Online communities, information sharing, and the ability to engage with one another are all made possible by social media platforms. Social networking sites like Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn, and TikTok are a few examples. User-generated content is a concept that social media platforms are frequently based on, which means that users of the platform are also the ones who produce the content that is shared there. Text postings, images, moving images, and connections to other websites might all fall under this category. Many people now consider social media to be a vital aspect of modern life, and it has significantly changed how we exchange information and communicate. It has also had an impact on politics by giving people a forum to organize, discuss topics, and sway public opinion. However, it has also come under fire for its propensity to erode privacy and contribute to polarization of society, as well as for the dissemination of false information and fake news.

Statistics of Social Media

• As of 2021, there are more than 4.2 billion active social media users worldwide, representing about 55% of the global population.

• The most popular social media platform is Facebook, with more than 2.7 billion active users.

• Instagram has more than 1 billion active users, while Twitter has more than 330 million and TikTok has more than 500 million.

• Social media users spend an average of 2 hours and 24 minutes per day on these platforms.

• Majority of social media users are under the age of 35, with a significant proportion being between the ages of 18 and 24.

• Media use is more common among women than men, with about 62% of women using these platforms compared to about 48% of men.

• The United States, about 72% of adults use social media, with the majority of users being between the ages of 18 and 29.
